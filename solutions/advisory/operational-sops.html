Operational SOP Definition

Processes that scale beyond any single employee.

Workflow maps, role-specific checklists, training materials, and onboarding playbooks. The tribal knowledge becomes documented operations — and your next hire is productive in days, not weeks.

Build Our SOPs What's included
The Problem

Independent practices run on tribal knowledge — and the front-desk labor market is collapsing under it.

One person knows the schedule template. Another knows the eligibility quirks for the three biggest payers. A third holds the recall list in their head. When any of them leave — and the average front-desk tenure in independent practice is now under 18 months — the practice starts over.

Meanwhile, the front-desk pay benchmark is $17–18/hour against a retail market clearing $22/hour. Turnover is structural now. The only durable response is to turn the tribal knowledge into documented operations — so the role survives the person.

What's Included

From the first ring of the phone to the last patient out the door.

We build the SOPs around your actual workflows — not a generic template. The deliverable is yours to edit, print, share, and revise as your practice evolves.

Workflow Maps

Every front-desk process visualized end-to-end — phone intake, scheduling, eligibility, check-in, check-out, recall. The map shows what happens, who owns it, and where the handoffs live. Editable visual format for your wall and your training binder.

Role Checklists

Step-by-step playbooks for each front-desk role — opening procedure, daily call workflow, eligibility decision tree, escalation paths. Includes phone scripts, the exact language for difficult conversations, and the "if/then" logic for recurring scenarios.

Training Materials

Onboarding curriculum for new front-desk hires. Day-one through day-five reading, shadowing milestones, role-play scenarios, and competency checks. By day five, the new hire has been measured against the same operating standard everyone else holds.

The Library

A complete operating library for your front desk.

Opening & closing procedures
Step-by-step morning startup and evening shutdown, with the exact order, the EHR queues to check, and the handoff to the next shift.
Phone scripts for the top 20 scenarios
Booking, rescheduling, insurance questions, refill requests, billing disputes, after-hours triage — every recurring conversation with the exact words that work.
Eligibility decision trees
"If primary payer is X, then Y" logic for the top 10 payers in your area. Removes the "we'll figure it out at checkout" pattern that creates denials.
Escalation paths
Who to hand which conversation to, when, and how. Eliminates the "let me get my manager" delay that turns simple questions into 20-minute incidents.
The Outcome
"Your front desk role becomes a repeatable system, not a person. When someone leaves, the role survives. When you hire, day-five productivity is the new baseline."
How It Works

Four weeks. End to end.

1

Week 1 · Observation + interview

If you've already done the Evaluation Framework, we skip to the SOP work. If not, we spend two days on-site capturing the workflows as they currently run — what's actually happening, not what the org chart says.

2

Weeks 2–3 · Drafting

We draft the workflow maps, role checklists, phone scripts, eligibility decision trees, and onboarding curriculum. You review iteratively — these are your operations.

3

Week 4 · Rollout + training

We deliver the final library and run a half-day training session with your front-desk team. Everyone leaves knowing where the SOPs live, how to use them, and how to revise them.

4

Ongoing · You own it

The library is yours. You edit it as your practice evolves. We're happy to do quarterly reviews if you want, but they're optional — the documents stand on their own.
