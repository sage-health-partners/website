The Problem

Your front desk spends 60% of their time on work that doesn't require a human.

Answering the same phone questions. Reading appointment confirmations off a printed sheet. Pulling up eligibility one chart at a time. The patient-facing work — the warmth, the judgment, the upset family member who needs a person — gets squeezed into the margins of the day, because the repetitive work consumes the hours.

For a four-physician practice, that's $180K–$240K per year of front-desk salary spent on tasks an autonomous agent now does as well or better.

Agents that work the workflow — end to end, not in fragments.

The Practice Agentic Suite is not a chatbot bolted onto your website. It's an operating layer that sits between the patient and your EHR, executing the full administrative workflow autonomously — from the first ring of the phone to the eligibility record in the chart.

Patient → Agent → EHR.
That's the whole architecture.

Every interaction follows the same flow: the patient does what they've always done — calls, texts, shows up — and the agent does the rest. Your EHR stays the source of truth. We integrate alongside it, not on top of it.

1

The patient initiates

A patient calls to book, replies to a confirmation SMS, or hits any of the other intake channels we cover. From their perspective, nothing about the interaction has changed.

2

The agent handles the conversation

The voice or messaging agent runs the conversation autonomously — asks the right questions, reads the patient's intent, and works the workflow it was deployed for. If the conversation goes outside the agent's scope, it hands off to your team with full context.

3

The EHR gets updated in real time

Appointments are booked, eligibility records are written, copay notes are added — directly to your EHR through native API where supported or RPA bridge where not. Your front desk sees the updates as they happen.

4

Practice BI captures the outcome

Every interaction is logged to the dashboard. Call volume, no-show recovery, eligibility hit rate — visible in real time, summarized in the monthly business review, never reliant on someone remembering to pull the report.
