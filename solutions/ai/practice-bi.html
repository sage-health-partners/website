The Problem

Practice owners are flying blind on the metrics that move revenue.

The EHR has reports. The phone system has reports. The clearinghouse has reports. They live in different systems, get pulled manually, arrive monthly, and almost nobody reads them once they're past the second page. By the time a problem shows up in a quarterly review, it has already cost the practice three months of revenue.

Decisions get made on gut feel — not because the data isn't there, but because nobody has a single place to see it. The independent practice runs the operational complexity of a small hospital with the visibility of a corner store.

What You'll See

The metrics that actually move revenue.

Practice BI ships with a curated set of metrics — the ones we have found consistently predict the difference between a healthy independent practice and one that's leaking cash. You can add custom metrics anytime.

Phone answer rate
Live and trended across peak hours, with the cost of every unanswered call quantified. Drill down by time of day to see when you're under-staffed.
No-show & cancellation trends
By provider, by visit type, by day of week. See which combinations have the highest no-show rates and which mitigation is working.
Eligibility verification rate
% of upcoming appointments with verified benefits. Plan-change detection rate. Denial-rework volume — the leading indicator of revenue at risk.
Point-of-service copay collection
% of copays collected at checkout vs. mailed-statement chasing. Trended by provider and time-of-day, with the dollar gap to benchmark surfaced.
Patient flow & wait time
Door-to-room, room-to-provider, provider-to-checkout. The flow metrics that reveal where the bottleneck is — not the one staff complain about.
Google review trends
New review count, average rating, recurring sentiment themes. The early-warning system for patient experience problems before they hit a survey.
